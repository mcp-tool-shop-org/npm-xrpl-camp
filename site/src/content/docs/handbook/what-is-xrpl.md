You do not need to understand blockchain to start XRPL Camp. But if you are curious, this page explains the basics in plain language — no jargon, no hype.

## The one-sentence version

The XRP Ledger is a public notebook that records who sent money to whom, and nobody can erase a page.

## A longer explanation

Imagine a spreadsheet that thousands of computers around the world keep a copy of. When someone sends money, every copy updates at the same time. Nobody owns the spreadsheet, and nobody can quietly change a number. That is a blockchain — and the XRP Ledger is one of the oldest and fastest ones.

A few things make it different from blockchains you may have heard of:

### It settles in 3–5 seconds

Bitcoin transactions can take 10 minutes or more to confirm. Ethereum is faster but still takes around 12 seconds. The XRP Ledger finalizes a transaction in 3–5 seconds. When XRPL Camp says "your payment is confirmed," it really is — in the time it takes to pour a coffee.

### It costs almost nothing

A typical XRPL transaction costs about 0.00001 XRP — a fraction of a fraction of a penny. During your training, the Testnet faucet gives you free test XRP, so you will not spend any real money at all.

### No mining

Bitcoin uses enormous amounts of electricity because computers compete to solve puzzles ("mining"). The XRP Ledger does not mine. Instead, trusted servers called **validators** agree on each new batch of transactions through a voting process. This is faster, cheaper, and uses far less energy.

### It was built for payments

Some blockchains try to do everything — smart contracts, NFTs, decentralized apps. The XRP Ledger was designed from the ground up to move money between people, companies, and currencies. It has a built-in exchange (the DEX) and native support for issuing tokens.

## Key words you will see during training

| Term | What it means |
|------|---------------|
| **Wallet** | Your identity on the ledger — a pair of keys (one public, one private) that let you send and receive XRP |
| **Address** | The public half of your wallet. Think of it like an email address — safe to share |
| **Secret (seed)** | The private half. Like a password — never share it |
| **Testnet** | A practice copy of the XRP Ledger. Everything works the same, but the money is not real |
| **Faucet** | A free dispenser that gives you test XRP on the Testnet so you can practice |
| **Transaction** | An instruction recorded on the ledger, like "send 10 XRP from address A to address B" |
| **Reserve** | A small amount of XRP (10 on Testnet) that your wallet must hold to exist on the ledger |
| **Validated** | The network has agreed this transaction is final — it cannot be reversed or changed |

## Why learn this?

You do not need to become a blockchain developer. But understanding how digital ledgers work is becoming a useful life skill — the same way understanding email or databases became useful decades ago.

After XRPL Camp, you will be able to:

- Explain what a blockchain does (and does not do) in a job interview
- Read a transaction on a public ledger and understand what happened
- Tell the difference between real blockchain projects and hype
- Decide if you want to go deeper — build apps, explore DeFi, or just move on with a new mental model
